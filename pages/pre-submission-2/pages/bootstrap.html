When You *Should* Use Bootstrap

A friendly reminder that Bootstrap is here to save you from writing actual CSS (but at what cost?).

You want to ship fast, but not care how it looks

Bootstrap lets you throw a navbar, jumbotron, and footer together in 10 minutes. Design? A problem for Future You.

You're okay looking like 90% of the web

Originality is overrated, right? Bootstrap’s default styles will make sure your site fits right in... with everyone else's.

You love adding 200kb of CSS for 2 buttons

Why write 10 lines of CSS when you can load an entire framework for a couple of rounded corners and a hover effect?

Download Spinner
You just started learning web dev yesterday

And you already deployed something with a modal, navbar, and accordion. Bootstrap makes it *feel* like you're a dev.

You want to avoid thinking about layout

Why deal with Grid or Flexbox when you can just slap on .row and .col and hope it works?

You miss the golden days of 2015 web design

Who needs modern design systems when you can have shadows, gradients, and buttons that scream “I was built with Bootstrap 3”?
